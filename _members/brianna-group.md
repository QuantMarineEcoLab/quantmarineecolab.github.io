Brianna manages the SOAR (Supporting Oyster Aquaculture and Restoration) program in New Hampshire, partnering with NHâ€™s shellfish industry on oyster restoration. She participates in regional meetings and committees and workgroups developing strategy and implementing projects to improve the health of the Great Bay and its watershed. She is a co-lead of the Great Bay 2030 Restore Critical Habitats Working group to advance the pace and scale of coastal habitat restoration and conservation. Brianna received her M.S. in Environmental Science and Management with a Conservation Planning Specialization from the Bren School at UC Santa Barbara. Brianna loves to hike, play volleyball and go on adventures with her dog Coco.
